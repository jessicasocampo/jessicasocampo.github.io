Real-World Readiness, Virtually Delivered

Overview

The project focused on developing an AR-based Cultural Adjustment and Language Learning app to support international and domestic undergraduate students. It aimed to address language barriers, cultural adaptation, and feelings of isolation by helping students practice real-life scenarios, build confidence in communication, and understand cultural nuances. The app also sought to reduce anxiety and mental health challenges, fostering smoother academic and social integration.

Context

International students often face language barriers, cultural adjustment challenges, and isolation, which can heighten stress and mental health concerns. These difficulties hinder their ability to practice language, navigate cultural norms, and feel supported, ultimately impacting academic performance, well-being, and community integration‚Äîunderscoring the need for tailored support tools.

Solution

The solution featured immersive AR scenarios simulating real-life settings, accessibility enhancements like voice commands and text-to-speech, and gamification elements such as XP rewards and progress tracking. A feedback system provided real-time insights, helping users practice language, adapt to cultural norms, reduce anxiety, and foster a sense of achievement.

Reflection

This project strengthened my skills in user-centered design, prototyping (Figma, Styly), and user research. I learned the importance of iteration, accessibility, and testing to improve usability. Challenges like navigation and balancing immersion with simplicity were solved by refining layouts, clarifying buttons, and streamlining features.
