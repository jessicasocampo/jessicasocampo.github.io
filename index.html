MAPLE LEAF FOOD'S INSPECTION FORM

Team: Jessica Ocampo & Jenani Yoganathan
Duration: 3 Months


Role:
UX RESEARCHER UX DESIGNER APP DEVELOPER

Tools:
#FIGMA #POWER APPS #CANVA


OVERVIEW

The project involved designing a tablet application prototype as part of Maple Leaf Foods’ "Path to Paperless" initiative. This application served as a universal form prototype/design system for employees to use at the plants, replacing paper forms such as inspection forms. It was developed exclusively for Maple Leaf Foods employees, specifically for plant workers. The goal was to reduce paper usage across Maple Leaf Foods by providing a digital solution that streamlined form-based tasks in an accessible and efficient way.


PROBLEM

Plant workers relied on paper-based forms, which were inefficient, environmentally unfriendly, and challenging to manage. Maple Leaf Foods wanted to digitize this process to align with their sustainability goals. Employees wearing gloves needed the application to be functional despite limited tactile precision. Employees who were less tech-savvy needed a user-friendly interface. The forms needed to balance simplicity and visual appeal while maintaining functionality. The paper-based process was resource-intensive, less environmentally sustainable, and prone to inefficiencies. Digitization offered a way to enhance operational effectiveness and align with Maple Leaf Foods’ sustainability goals.


PROCESS

  • Research: Conducted interviews and user testing with plant employees to understand their needs, workflows, and challenges with transitioning to digital forms.

  • Ideation: Brainstormed solutions collaboratively using Figma to create low-fidelity wireframes, which evolved into high-fidelity prototypes. Canva was used to source icons to enhance the visual design.


    • This is the original design which got scrapped due to a new criteria so have no logo or mlf colours.


  • Design: Focused on creating simple yet visually appealing forms with key features such as dark mode and light mode. The high-fidelity designs were built in Figma.

  • Testing: Validated designs through usability testing with employees to ensure they were intuitive and functional, even for users with gloves or limited tech experience. Prototypes were then built in Microsoft Power Apps for further testing.


This is the final product.

SOLUTION

A simple, user-friendly interface. Support for dark mode and light mode to cater to user preferences and visual comfort. Compatibility with glove use to accommodate plant workers. Icons and visual elements to enhance clarity and usability. The design addressed user needs by being intuitive and accessible for a wide range of employees, reducing the learning curve for older or less tech-savvy workers. It eliminated paper dependency, aligning with the company’s sustainability objectives.


IMPACT

  • Results: The prototype was handed off to the development team for implementation. It set the foundation for a scalable, reusable design system for other form-based applications across Maple Leaf Foods.

  • What was the outcome? Positioned the company to significantly reduce paper usage at plants, contributing to their "Path to Paperless" initiative.

  • Measurable metrics: While specific metrics were not mentioned, the project advanced Maple Leaf Foods’ sustainability and digital transformation goals.

WHAT DID I LEARN?

  • How to work cross-functionally with various teams and stakeholders.

  • Best practices for designing tablet applications, especially for industrial use cases.

  • The importance of user-centric design in making technology accessible.


Challenges I faced and how I overcame them.

  • One challenge involved designing for users who wear gloves. The solution was to simplify touchpoints and conduct usability testing while users were wearing gloves.

  • Another challenge was ensuring the application appealed to older, less tech-savvy employees. To address this, simplicity was prioritized, and the application was extensively tested with this user group.

Skills Gained:

  • Developed proficiency in Figma for design and prototyping, gained experience with Microsoft Power Apps for low-fidelity interactive prototypes, and enhanced understanding of design standards for iPad/tablet applications.

CROSSCULTURA

Team: Jessica Ocampo, Isabelle Cho, Jackie Zhang
Duration: 2 Months


Role:
UX RESEARCHER UX DESIGNER STYLY DEVELOPER

Tools:
#FIGMA #STYLY #PREMIERE PRO

SHORT VIDEO

OVERVIEW

The project focused on developing a Cultural Adjustment and Language Learning App to support international students in overcoming language barriers, cultural adjustment challenges, and feelings of isolation. The app was designed for international undergraduate students as the primary target audience, addressing their unique challenges.

The goal was to create an augmented reality app that helps international students improve language skills, understand cultural nuances, and practice real-life scenarios. The app aimed to alleviate anxiety and mental health challenges, fostering better academic and social integration.


PROBLEM

International students often struggle with language barriers, adapting to cultural norms and feelings of isolation, which intensify stress and mental health challenges. These difficulties make it harder for students to practice academic and conversational language, navigate cultural etiquette, and feel supported in their new environments. As a result, their academic performance, well-being, and ability to integrate into their communities suffer, highlighting a critical need for tailored mental health support and adjustment tools to address these unique challenges.


PROCESS

  • Research: Methods Used: Think-aloud protocol, System Usability Scale (SUS) surveys, semi-structured interviews, and user interaction analytics. Feedback emphasized accessibility, intuitive use, and engaging features.

  • Ideation: Brainstormed features like immersive scenarios (e.g., grocery shopping, lectures), NPC interactions, and gamification elements (XP points, levels).


    • This is the brand style guideline.


  • Design: Tools: Figma and Styly for high-fidelity prototypes. Implemented Universal Design principles, ensuring accessibility (e.g., high-contrast modes, text-to-speech, and voice commands).

  • Testing: Validation Methods: User testing provided qualitative and quantitative insights. Issues like navigation, clarity of interface elements, and the lack of interactivity were identified and iterated upon.


This is the iterated prototype.

SOLUTION

The solution featured immersive AR scenarios simulating real-life settings, accessibility enhancements like voice commands and text-to-speech, and gamification elements such as XP rewards and progress tracking. A feedback system provided real-time insights, helping users practice language, adapt to cultural norms, reduce anxiety, and foster a sense of achievement.


IMPACT

  • Results: The app demonstrated improved usability, with SUS scores indicating it was user-friendly, achieving a mean ease-of-use score of 3.75. Users provided positive feedback, praising features such as transcripts, adjustable audio speeds, and XP rewards for supporting mental health and enhancing engagement. Additionally, the simulated environments helped reduce anxiety by making learning less intimidating.

  • What was the outcome? Users appreciated the app’s immersive and gamified approach, encouraging frequent use. Participants suggested the potential for increased student confidence and improved academic/social adjustment.

  • Measurable metrics: Average SUS score: 74 (indicative of good usability). Reduced perceived complexity: Low complexity score (mean 1.5).

WHAT DID I LEARN?

  • The project highlighted the importance of user-centered design and the value of continuous iteration based on feedback. Incorporating accessibility and inclusivity was shown to enhance usability and increase user satisfaction. Additionally, user testing proved invaluable for uncovering hidden pain points and identifying areas for improvement.


Challenges I faced and how I overcame them.

  • One challenge was ensuring intuitive navigation and interactivity within the app. This was addressed by adding headers, improving button clarity, and enhancing navigation paths. Another challenge involved balancing immersion and simplicity, which was resolved by streamlining unnecessary features, such as removing confusing visualizers.

Skills Gained:

  • The project enhanced proficiency in prototyping tools like Figma and Styly. It also developed skills in conducting user research and analyzing both qualitative and quantitative data. Additionally, it emphasized applying Universal Design principles to ensure accessibility for all users.

MOODLE

Team: Jessica Ocampo, Cindy Choung, Joy Li, Cici Chen, Sophia Ho, Collin Yeung
Duration: 6 hours


Role:
UX RESEARCHER UX DESIGNER GRAPHIC DESIGNER

Tools:
#FIGMA #PROCREATE #ILLUSTRATOR

OVERVIEW

The project was about designing a mobile application aimed at raising awareness and reducing the stigma around mental health for pre-teens. It featured educational and engaging components to help foster a better understanding of mental health in a fun, interactive way. The application was developed for the Sun Life UXperience Designathon. The goal was to create an engaging and educational tool to help pre-teens understand mental health better, raise awareness, and combat the stigma surrounding it.


PROBLEM

The key problem was the lack of accessible, engaging resources aimed at reducing the stigma around mental health for pre-teens. Mental health awareness is often overlooked at a younger age, especially for those in the pre-teen demographic. The primary challenge was that pre-teens often don’t have access to or understanding of mental health resources that are both appropriate and engaging for their age. This could lead to misunderstandings or stigma around mental health. For Sun Life and the UXperience Designathon, addressing mental health stigma in the younger demographic could help in shaping a more informed, compassionate generation and strengthen the community's well-being and engagement.


PROCESS

  • Research: We began by researching existing mobile health applications to analyze trends, features, and targeting user demographics. This helped ensure the app’s feasibility, usability, and engagement potential.

  • Ideation: We kicked off the design process with brainstorming sessions to generate ideas, followed by group discussions to refine the concept. This collaborative approach helped us focus on features that would resonate with the pre-teen demographic.

This is a drawing of our low-fidelity.

  • Design: We used tools like Figma for creating the low-fidelity sketches and prototypes, and Procreate for visual design elements. Our graphic designers worked on the visual aesthetics, while researchers ensured the design remained cohesive and appropriate for the target audience.

  • Testing: We took the opportunity to consult with mentors during the event, who provided valuable insights that helped us edit and improve our designs and concepts. This feedback was crucial in validating our design choices and enhancing the app’s overall appeal and usability.


This is our brainstorming & prototype file.

SOLUTION

The design featured interactive educational content, an easy-to-navigate interface, and visuals tailored to pre-teens. We focused on creating a fun and informative experience. By providing a non-intimidating space to learn about mental health, the design directly addressed the stigma surrounding it, helping pre-teens understand the topic in an accessible way.


IMPACT

The project received positive feedback during the pitch, with judges praising the engaging and educational design. Although there were no measurable metrics due to the 6-hour time limit, the judges appreciated the balance of usability, aesthetics, and educational value. They also provided constructive feedback, which helped refine our concept. As our first designathon, there was room for improvement, and we took the insights to enhance our future design processes.

WHAT DID I LEARN?

  • I learned how to rapidly collaborate with a team to ideate, design, and prototype an application under tight time constraints.

  • It was also a valuable experience in understanding how to tailor solutions for specific user demographics.


Challenges I faced and how I overcame them.

  • The biggest challenge was working within the time limit. We overcame this by efficiently dividing tasks, with each team member focusing on their strengths—graphic design, research, and prototyping—while ensuring cohesive collaboration.

Skills Gained:

  • I gained experience in rapid prototyping with Figma, collaborative brainstorming, and research on health-related mobile apps. I also honed my skills in presenting and pitching concepts to judges.

SUSHI KUI'S MENU


Created by: Jessica Ocampo
Duration: 4 months


Role:
UX RESEARCHER UI DESIGNER GRAPHIC DESIGNER

Tools:
#FIGMA #PROCREATE #ILLUSTRATOR

OVERVIEW

The project involved redesigning the menu for Sushi Kui, a restaurant in Aurora. The focus was to create a more user-centric and competitive menu design by incorporating user research, competitor analysis, and principles of accessibility and usability. The goal was to improve the menu's usability and overall customer satisfaction through a redesign that reflected both the restaurant's brand identity and its business objectives. Additionally, the redesign was necessary due to changes in item prices, which needed to be integrated seamlessly into the new layout.


PROBLEM

The existing menu at Sushi Kui was not user-friendly, lacked a modern and accessible layout, and required the incorporation of updated prices for many items. Users struggled with navigating the old menu, which had poor visual hierarchy and lacked clarity, making it difficult for them to make quick decisions. This hindered overall dining satisfaction, especially considering the diverse clientele of young adults, middle-aged customers, and elders, who had varying levels of technological familiarity.

For the business, these issues led to suboptimal customer experiences, potentially reducing retention and satisfaction. Furthermore, the outdated design and pricing inconsistencies affected the restaurant’s competitiveness and could create confusion among customers.


PROCESS

  • Research: I conducted user research to identify pain points in the current menu and analyzed competitors' menus to understand industry standards. This helped inform strategic design decisions, ensuring the redesign would meet user needs and stand out in the competitive market. I also took into account the restaurant’s demographic of young adults, middle-aged customers, and elders to ensure the design was accessible and easy to navigate for a wide age range.

  • Ideation: I worked closely with the business owners to brainstorm ideas, ensuring the design aligned with both the brand's identity and their vision. They gave me a lot of creative freedom, but I made sure to incorporate their feedback throughout the process.


    • This is the original menu.


  • Design: Using Figma, I created a user-friendly menu layout that prioritized accessibility, readability, and visual appeal. I paid attention to the visual hierarchy, ensuring that customers could easily find key information. The design also included photos of the food, which I personally took, ensuring high-quality, appetizing images that aligned with the restaurant’s branding.

  • Testing: I conducted informal testing with a few regular customers to gather feedback on the new design, focusing on usability and ease of navigation. I iterated based on this feedback to refine the design before finalizing it.


This is the final product.

SOLUTION

The key features of the redesign included a streamlined layout with clearly defined sections, improved visual hierarchy, and high-quality food photography that aligned with the restaurant's brand identity. The design focused on enhancing the user experience by making the menu easier to read and navigate, with particular attention to accessibility for all age groups. The integration of updated prices was done in a clear, non-disruptive way.

This redesign significantly improved usability, making the menu easier to understand and navigate, which enhanced the overall dining experience. The updated prices and consistent branding ensured the menu felt cohesive and professional, while the focus on accessibility made it suitable for a wide range of customers.


IMPACT

The redesigned menu led to an increase in customer satisfaction, as the new layout was more intuitive and visually appealing. The updated pricing reduced confusion, while the improved design made the menu easier to navigate. Business owners also reported increased sales and stronger customer loyalty. Additionally, the redesign opened up opportunities to create a digital version of the menu accessible via QR code, offering a contactless, convenient option for customers and positioning the restaurant for future growth.

WHAT DID I LEARN?

  • I learned the importance of maintaining a balance between user-centric design and business objectives. Collaboration with the business owners was key to ensuring the design met their vision while addressing user needs.

  • Additionally, I learned that updating pricing and making it easy to integrate into the design is crucial for a smooth customer experience, especially when considering a broad age range of customers.


Challenges I faced and how I overcame them.

  • The main challenge was taking high-quality photos of the food, which took considerable time and effort. I overcame this by planning a dedicated photo session, ensuring the images were both visually appealing and aligned with the brand.

  • Additionally, ensuring that the pricing changes were incorporated seamlessly into the design required constant communication with the owners.

Skills Gained:

  • I gained skills in user research, UX/UI design, and collaboration with clients to ensure the final product met brand objectives. I learned to integrate pricing updates while maintaining usability and clarity. Additionally, I developed insight into age-inclusive design, making the menu accessible to a diverse customer base.
