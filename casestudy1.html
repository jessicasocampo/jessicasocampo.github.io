PROBLEM

Many individuals struggle to seek help for mental health as it is so stigmatized. However, it must be known that mental health is more important than it may seem. Many Canadians struggle to understand their mental health. While Youth and teens are confused and unaware about how to understand their feelings. It may be good to alert them early on as 70% of mental health problems have their onset during childhood or adolescence. Adolescents with mental health conditions are particularly vulnerable to social exclusion, discrimination, stigmatized.

GOAL & AUDIENCE


As a group, we planned to educate young teens early in life to bring awareness of mental health and to destigmatize the topic more. We wanted to create a guided, interactive platform for an easy educational experience.


Our target audience is for preteens who are finding an easy and engaging way to educate themselves on mental health. As we want to create a safe environment that feels comfortable for teens to learn in. This will provide them with a pathway to help them understand heavy topics and make it not seem taboo.


ROLE

Roles during the duration of the projects were split into the UX/UI researchers, UX/UI designers, and graphic designers. In this team-based project, my role was to work on the UX/UI elements regarding app layout, colour, interactivity.
